How Does ImPrint Work?

ImPrint works by shredding your clean plastic bottles (or any Type 1 plastics). It then uses a vacuum to transport the plastic shreds into a holding container. The 4mm delta extruder head melts down and extrudes the plastic shreds as it prints so that no separate extrusion is needed. Hooray for energy savings! The 4mm extruder head prints a thicker material which results in faster printing and more sturdy products.

Why is ImPrint more environmentally friendly?

Compared to Plastic Manufacturing

Traditional plastic manufacturing requires crude oil or natural gas and many energy and resources intensive processes that all go into us using a piece of plastic once. ImPrint gives new life to old plastic with simple mechanical processes including shredding, melting, and printing!

Compared to Standard Recycling:

Because ImPrint lives in your school, no transportation is required to recycle old products and acquire new ones! Think about how the recycling truck has to pick up your old plastic at your (and everyone else's) homes, drive to the recycling center, sort and clean the plastic, drive it to a manufacturer who then creates new products and transports them to stores where you have to drive to buy them. Whew! That's a long journey for a little bit of plastic.
